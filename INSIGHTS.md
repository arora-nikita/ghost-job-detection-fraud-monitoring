# Key Insights from Ghost Job Detection Project

## 1. Salary Information is a Strong Fraud Indicator
Jobs without salary details showed a significantly higher probability of being fraudulent compared to jobs with clear salary ranges.

## 2. Risk Level Classification Effectively Separates Fraudulent Posts
The Ghost Score successfully categorized jobs into Safe, Suspicious, and Likely Ghost, where most fraudulent jobs fell into the "Likely Ghost" category.

## 3. Certain Countries Show Higher Concentration of Suspicious Jobs
Fraudulent and high-risk job postings were concentrated in specific countries, indicating regional patterns in fake listings.

## 4. Employment Type Plays a Role in Fraud
Jobs with missing or vague employment types had a higher association with fraud compared to clearly defined roles.

## 5. Keyword-Based Detection Helps Identify Ghost Jobs
Job descriptions containing phrases like "quick money", "no experience", and "earn fast" were frequently linked with fraudulent postings.

## 6. Machine Learning Model Identified Important Fraud Drivers
Random Forest feature importance showed that salary missing, keyword count, and description length were among the top predictors of fraud.
